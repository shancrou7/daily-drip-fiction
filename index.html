Day 43 June 4, 2026
The mechanical voice echoing through the receiver matched the dead tone of her childhood tracker perfectly. Sarah’s knuckles went pale against the plastic console, her boots sinking millimetres deeper into the sub-zero blue mist creeping along the vault floor. She demanded answers, but only standard system static returned her cry. Suddenly, the single stone pedestal holding the phone shuttered violently, sinking rapidly into the steel ground beneath her. A hidden floor grate slid back to expose a sheer drop into a labyrinth of live high-voltage wiring. One wrong step meant execution. As the platform gave its final lurch, the heavy vault door behind her slammed shut, locking automatically with a permanent, hydraulic hiss.
Day 42 June 3, 2026
Yesterday, the vault door finally nudged open. But it didn't reveal gold. Instead, a freezing, blue mist poured over Sarah’s boots, smelling faintly of ozone and old paper. Inside, a single stone pedestal held a rotary telephone. Suddenly, it began to ring. Sarah froze. No one had this number. She picked up the receiver. The voice on the other end whispered her childhood nickname—and the line went dead.
